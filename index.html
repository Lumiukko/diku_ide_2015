2. Interactive Visualizations

2.1 Gapminder World, by Hans Rosling et al.

Gapminder.org

Gapminder was developed by Prof. Hans Rosling and his teamt to display the development of health and wealth across the past few decades for a multitude of countries. The diagram shows each country as a circle, where the size of the circle corresponds to the size of its population, the position on the x-axis corresponds to the wealth, and the position on the y-axis corresponds to health. The time progression over the years is additionally shown as an animation.

The original visualization contained interactive elements. For example, each country could be seperated into its regions by clicking on the corresponding circle. Furthermore, the time animation can be controlled with a slider widget.
